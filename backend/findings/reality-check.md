# Reality Check — What the City Already Has vs. What We Add

**Research date:** June 26, 2026

---

## Existing Infrastructure

| System | Coverage | Gap |
|--------|----------|-----|
| **DWD weather stations** | City-wide (1-2 stations) | Reports airport conditions. A tree on a south-facing paved street can be 5-8°C hotter. No per-street microclimate. |
| **Visual inspections** | Drive-by, sporadic | "That tree looks dry." Reactive, not predictive. After wilting is too late. |
| **Citizen complaints** | Ad-hoc | "The tree outside my window looks dead." Biased toward visible/high-traffic trees. |
| **Fixed watering schedule** | All registered trees | Waters trees that don't need it. Skips trees that do. Zero data feedback loop. |
| **Baumpatenschaft program** | Volunteer-based | Exists (citizens can sponsor trees) but no monitoring — they water on honor system. |
| **GIS tree registry** | All city trees | Has locations and species. No real-time health data. Static. |

## What We Add

| Layer | Current State | With Our Platform |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Per-tree moisture** | None | Real-time soil moisture at root level |
| **Microclimate** | Airport station only | Per-tree temp, humidity, light exposure |
| **Citizen coordination** | Honor system, no feedback | Push alerts, leaderboard, verified watering |
| **Predictive modeling** | None | "This tree will be critical in 6 hours at current rate" |
| **Cost tracking** | Aggregate budget only | Per-tree cost of watering, per-tree savings from citizen labor |

## Honest Assessment

**Weakness:** Tree protection (guards, pruning, root management) costs more than watering and isn't addressed by sensors. Be ready for this question.

**Strength:** The city spends €1M/year watering on a fixed schedule with zero data feedback. Even a 20% reduction in wasted watering pays for the sensor network in year one. And the real win — preventing mature tree death — is worth €5k-15k per tree.

**Pitch framing:** Don't say "we save money on water." Say "we save trees that would otherwise die in the next heat wave — and that saves millions."
